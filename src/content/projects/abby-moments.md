## The Brief

Abby is a museum in Kortrijk that had recently reopened after renovations. They wanted to attract new visitors, but more importantly, they wanted to boost participation in activities. The challenge: how do you encourage people to participate in events when there's a barrier to entry, especially for smaller activities?

## The Concept: Abbymomenten

Our team pitched two concepts. The client chose one, and together we developed it into **Abbymomenten**, a campaign built on a simple insight: *If you can break the barrier with small activities, bigger participation follows.*

The campaign encourages people to **slow down with Abby** and start with small, low-pressure moments. These micro-activities lower the barrier to entry, making it easier for visitors to eventually participate in bigger events. The centerpiece is the **momentenmuur** (moments wall), a shared display where visitors can see activities others have created or participated in, building community and FOMO in a positive way.

## What We Built

A fully functional website where visitors can:
- [-] **Organize events** (small or big)
- [-] **Participate** in museum activities or visitor-created events
- [-] **Share their moment** on the momentenmuur wall
- [-] See what others are doing on the website or the physical wall in the museum, creating a sense of community and inspiration

## My Role: Lead Developer & Database Architect

As lead developer, I was responsible for:

- [-] **Frontend Development** - Building the React website with a focus on encouraging participation and exploration
- [-] **Physical Prototyping** - Creating a physical version of the momentenmuur wall by using an Arduino.
- [-] **System Architecture** - Designing the architecture of the whole system, so everything kept synched and working properly together.
- [-] **Supabase** to handle user data, event data, and the momentenmuur information
- [-]**Collaboration** - Working closely with the designer who created the visual language, the lead UXer who shaped strategy, and other developers

## The Process

The ideation phase was collaborative with lots of debating, sketching, and refining to land on one concept everyone believed in. Once locked in, we divided roles:
- [-] I handled the technical foundation and backend
- [-] Our designer created the visual identity and app design
- [-] Our lead UXer focused on strategy and experience
- [-] Everyone supported each other as needed

This structure allowed us to move fast while staying aligned on the vision.
